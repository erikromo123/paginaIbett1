CAF

• Capgemini Agile Framework (CAF) provides guidance and best practices for efficient execution of our agile engagements, is a collaborative step within Capgemini towards speaking one common agile language and living one common agile way of working.
• CAF includes the lifecycle, together with the tools to be used on agile engagements.
• CAF is lightweight, based on Scrum, extended with eXtreme Programming (XP) practices and complemented by Capgemini’s best practices. It can be tailored based on the client needs.

Our Capgemini Agile Framework

Capgemini Agile Framework (CAF) is a lightweight hybrid framework based on Scrum and is extended with eXtreme programming practices. It is complimented by our own proven best practices as appropriate for large scale system integrator like Capgemini. CAF is aligned to Agile Manifesto and its principles.

•Individuals and Interactions over processes and tools
•Working Software over comprehensive documentation
•Customer Collaboration over contract negotiation
•Responding to Change over following a plan

Scale

The scale chapter provides guidelines and practices for effectively and efficiently delivering large sized agile Projects. CAF Scale chapter provides the approach and best practices for delivering large sized agile engagements.

Distributed

CAF Distributed chapter provides guidance and best practices to engagements operating in a distributed environment, and on how to operate and create greater value in rightshore environment.

Pre Sales

CAF Pre-Sales chapter provides guidance and best practices that can be used in the Sales/Pre-Sales phases. It provides support Bid Managers and the pre-Sales team and specific guidance on how to write Agile contracts.






Lifecycle

CAF Lifecycle chapter provides a lightweight approach, based on Scrum and extended with eXtreme Programming (XP) and other agile best practices for agile delivery.FlyingStart which helps accelerate mobilization of agile projects by leveling the team’s understanding. CAF Lifecycle includes an initial Prepare, followed by iterative Build, Release & Deploy.

Tools

CAF Tools chapter provides a selection criteria around key standard tools that can be embraced by an engagement depending on its needs.

Governance

CAF Governance chapter provides a model for administering an agile engagement and the key metrics (agile specific) that can be used to track and control delivery.

Why have a Capgemini Agile Framework?

Agile: Capgemini sees Agile as the best way to deliver for our customers.
Continuous learning: A common language and a common framework are a prerequisite for continuous learning and improvement across the diverse geographies and customers that Capgemini serves.
Minimum set:With CAF we are establishing a minimum set of proven practices to successfully deliver agile projects across all industries and sizes in true partnership with our customers
framework and language to drive and spread that improvement : increase our people engagement, growth and capability, strengthen the effectiveness of our delivery to our clients,
DevOps Oportunity:Acts as an enabler for DevOps.

CAF Ethos

• Simplicity: We will do what is needed and asked for, but no more. This will maximize the value created for the investment made to date. We will take small simple steps to reach our goal and mitigate failures as they appen.
• Communication: Everyone is part of the team and we communicate daily. We will work together on everything from requirements to code. We will create the best solution to address our problem.
• Feedback:We will take every iteration commitment seriously by delivering working software.We demonstrate our software early and take the feedback with utmost importance to understand any changes needed. We will talk about the project and adapt our process to it, not the other way around.
• Respect: Everyone gives and feels the respect they deserve as a valued team member. Everyone contributes value even if it's simply enthusiasm. Developers respect the expertise of the customers and vice versa. Management respects our right to accept responsibility and receive authority over our own work.,
• Courage: We will tell the truth about progress and estimates. We don't document excuses for failure because we plan to succeed. We don't fear anything because no one ever works alone. We will adapt to changes when ever they happen

CAF Principles

At Capgemini, we strongly support and stand by the agile principles laid down in the agile manifesto:
1.- • Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
2.-Welcome changing requirements, even late in development. Agile processes harness change for the customer's competitive advantage.
3.- Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with a preference to the shorter timescale.
4.- Business people and developers must work together daily throughout the project
5.- Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done.
6.- • The most efficient and effective method of conveying information to and within a development team is face-to-face conversation.
7.- Working software is the primary measure of progress.
8.- Agile processes promote sustainable development.The sponsors, developers, and users should be able to maintain a constant pace indefinitely.
9.- TContinuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility.
10.- Simplicity--the art of maximizing the amount of work not done--is essential.
11.- The best architectures, requirements, and designs come from teams being facilitated by a collaborative architect.
12.- At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly.
